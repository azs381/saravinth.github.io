Compressible shear layer dynamics of compression-ramp type geometries in high-speed flows

The thermo-structural response of a high-speed vehicle at concave/convex junctions (due to shock-wave boundary layer interaction) is excited by the unsteady flow in the low-frequency spectrum. They overlap with the structural frequencies compelling the need to understand the causal mechanism of the fluid system. Unfortunately, the space-time decomposition of such flow features often requires a long runtime, and complex geometries, such as the cone-slice-ramp comprise a high-dimensional state space making it computationally intractable. The linear operators of local and bi-global coordinates are probed to extract the unstable modes and establish physical mechanism driving the fluid unsteadiness.

  • Sadagopan, A., Huang, D., Pandey, A., Casper, K., DeChant, L., “Compressible shear layer dynamics of a cone-slice-ramp in high-speed flows”, under review.
Fluid-thermal structural interaction of cone-slice-ramp in hypersonic flows

We explore the aerothermoelastic behavior of a complex geometry, i.e., cone-slice-ramp, in the air-breathing hypersonic regime. The objective is to discover the interaction mechanism of the multi-physics problem and develop reduced-order models for multi-disciplinary design evaluation and trajectory prediction. The challenge lies in resolving the \(10^9\) order of magnitude difference in the temporal decomposition of the events across fluid, and thermo-structural domains, which makes it computationally intractable to predict the aerothermoelastic behavior of a complex geometry of the entire flight trajectory (~\(10\) min). A partition-based explicitly coupled high-fidelity numerical framework is validated against the experimental data for FTSI analysis of the cone-slice-ramp. The results revealed a fluid-dominant one-way coupling for the high ramp angle case. Based on this, a simplified thermos-structural solver is developed based on heat equation (shell-element based FEM) and modal solver (Rayleigh-Ritz).

  • Sadagopan, A., Huang, D., Jirasek, A., Seidel, J., Pandey, A., Casper, K., “ Hypersonic Fluid–Thermal–Structural Interaction of Cone–Slice–Ramp: Computations with Experimental Validation,” AIAA Journal, 2023. DOI:10.2514/1.J062326
Computational validation of the PSU AFTRF single stage high-pressure turbine

Efficiency of a gas-turbine engine is critical given its worldwide usage across several sectors. Its performance is directly proportional to operational costs, fuel consumption and environmental sustainability. Especially in aviation, even a \(1\%\) improvement will lead to savings in several millions of dollars. In this context, the tip leakage contributes roughly \(1/3^{th}\) of overall turbine aerodynamic loss. Therefore, the objective was to develop efficient tip designs to improve the design efficiency. Leveraging computational fluid dynamics, the computational model of a high-pressure (HP) Turbine stage at the PSU-AFTRF experimental facility was validated using steady-state RANS. The computational model was used to characterize aerothermal losses due to tip leakage, and secondary flows in the blade passage. The location of boundary layer transition on the nozzle guide vane was identified as a key factor to accurately predict the tip leakage flow.

Design of a high-pressure ratio mixed flow compressor for small jet-engines

We propose a single-stage high-pressure ratio compressor using first-order principles to address the surging demand in the small aircraft engine segment (1-10 kg/s). The design is optimized, validated and examined for the flow physics with the RANS-based CFD in STAR-CCM+. A design point pressure ratio of 5.83 with 75.5% efficiency at 3.5Kg/s was achieved within 0.4m frontal diameter (higher performance than existing MFCs in the open literature).

  • Sadagopan, A., Camci, C., “A design strategy for a 6:1 supersonic mixed flow compressor”, Aerospace Science and Technology, Elsevier, 2019. DOI:10.1016/j.ast.2019.02.026
  • Sadagopan, A., Camci, C., “Viscous flow and performance issues in a 6:1 supersonic mixed-flow compressor with a tandem diffuser”, Aerospace Science and Technology, Elsevier, 2019. DOI:10.1016/j.ast.2019.02.027
